EVENT BEGINS IN

Experience Webbstock

Music. Art. Food. Experiences. Community. Surprises.

Gardner-Webb University and the Town of Boiling Springs are shutting down Main Street to bring you the experience of jammin’ music, good vibes, thrilling moments, and lifelong memories.

The March 28 event starts at 3 p.m. and will include live performances by top local and regional music artists, food vendors, handmade goods by artisans, kid zone activities, a firework display to conclude the event, and the Broad River Greenway will host a beverage garden for attendees 21+.

Plan your day at #Webbstock—Explore the festival map.

Performances by



Acoustic Syndicate is a true rock/folk/bluegrass band. North Carolina natives, the band formed in 1992, and their sound is characterized by tight three-part vocal harmonies, blazing guitar picking, complex polyrhythmic banjo playing, backed by a rock-steady rhythm section.
Black Water Rhythm & Blues is beach music band based out of Clarkton, NC. The band performs top 40, blues, funk and country, and every performance is an experience to be enjoyed by all.
Scott is a singer/songwriter from Shelby, NC. A blend of acoustic rock flavored with Americana, Scott Moss and the $100 Handshakes share the story of growing up in this all-American town with a striking, and sometimes gritty honesty.
Love and Valor is an emerging Americana-flavored Folk band based out of Burlington, NC. The band has been a staple in the NC folk scene since 2012, and are known for performing almost exclusively original music.
Too Raw - T.J. is the life of the party naturally. When he gets in his own element of art through dancing, you are guaranteed to be engaged and entertained.
Tonal Spectrum is Crest High School’s premiere a cappella group from Shelby, NC.
Gardner-Webb alumna Kelli Bartik brings the latest from the world of sports throughout the week as a sports anchor for WCCB News. Kelly will bring the energy as Webbtock’s emcee.


Info

When and where will the festival be held?

Webbstock will take place March 28, 2020 on South Main Street in Boiling Springs, NC.

Who is performing this year?

Stage Schedule


3-4 p.m. Love & Valor

4-4:30 p.m. Tonal Spectrum

5-6 p.m. BlackWater Band

6-6:20 p.m. TooRaw (street dancer)

6:30 p.m. Scott Moss and the $100 Handshakes

8:00 p.m. Acoustic Syndicate

9:15 p.m. Fireworks

What will be the format of the event?

The event is free to attend, and attendees can come in go throughout the duration of the festival. Webbstock kicks off at 3 p.m. with live performances by top local and regional music artists, food vendors, handmade goods by artisans, and kid zone activities. A firework display to conclude the event, and the Broad River Greenway will host a beverage garden for attendees 21+.

Is this event family friendly?

Bring the whole crew! Webbstock is a family-friendly event, and we welcome guests of all ages. Check out our Kid Zone for fun activities for children

Will there be Webbstock merchandise sold?

Yes, the following official Webbstock merchandise will be available at the festival, while supplies last:
  • T-shirts
  • Hats
  • Cups
  • Stickers

Will restrooms be available?

Yes, portable restrooms will be available at three locations.

What is being done to make the festival a safe, family-friendly environment?

The health and safety of every attendee is the number one goal of the festival organizers.  If you see anything about the design of the grounds or the operation of the festival that could be improved for purposes of safety, please let us know by contacting University Police at 704-406-4444.

Where is first aid located?

The First aid station is clearly marked on the Webbstock map and visible at the festival.

Will child care be provided

Child care will not be provided. Webbstock is a family-friendly event, and a kid zone will be set up for children to enjoy inflatables and other fun obstacles.

Where will alcohol be served?

Alcohol is designated to Broad River Beverage Garden and limited to attendees ages 21+ with event wristband.

What can't I bring with me?

  • Alcohol
  • Audio Recording Devices
  • Drones or Remote Control Aircraft, Toys or Cars
  • Drugs & Drug Paraphernalia
  • Fireworks or Explosives
  • Glass, Cans, Cups or Coolers
  • Instruments
  • Knives, Weapons, etc.
  • Laser Pointers
  • Pets
  • Selfie Sticks
  • Tobacco or Vapor Products

What should I bring with me?

  • Planning to enjoy the Broad River Beverage Garden —bring a government issued ID
  • A plan to get home safely
  • A fully charged cell phone (bring a portable charger too, if you've got one)
  • Water bottle
  • Rain poncho/umbrella if rainy weather is in the forecast (Webbstock will go on rain or shine)
  • Earplugs (it may get a little loud)
  • A hat, sunglasses and sunblock
  • Some cash (be sure to check out our delicious food vendors!)

Can I bring a camera?

Capture the moment! All cameras, including DSLRs, are permitted.

For the chance to be featured—#Webbstock

Can I bring a bag to the festival?

All personal items including purses, tote bags, backpacks and drawstring bags will be admitted into the festival without size restriction.

What is the cost to attend?

They say some of the greatest things in life are free—and Webbstock is one of them. Gardner-Webb University and the Town of Boiling Springs are proud to be able to host this event for the community free of cost for attendees.

Where is parking available?

Parking will be available in designated lots on Gardner-Webb’s campus map

Where is ADA parking?

Designated handicapped accessible parking will be available in the parking lot behind Webb Hall on Gardner-Webb’s campus map

Do I need cash?

Most vendors will accept credit cards. All Webbstock attendees are encouraged to have cash available. 

Are there ATMs on site?

ATM machines are located nearby at the CVS located at 105 E College Avenue as well as in the basement of the Tucker Student Center on Gardner-Webb’s campus.

What happens if it rains?

Webbstock is an outdoor event and is held rain or shine. The case of inclement or severe weather posing a threat to attendee safety, the festival may be delayed or cancelled and the festival grounds may be evacuated. We encourage attendees to check the weather forecast and dress accordingly (i.e. in the event of rain, we advise ponchos and rain boots.) Webbstock will not be rescheduled in the event of delay or cancellation.

Can I still be a vendor?

Apply to be a vendor; complete the application form.
